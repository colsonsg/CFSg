Questions worth asking before you apply

When and how does the Singapore cohort meet?

Twice a month — the 2nd and 4th Thursdays, 8–10pm SGT — over Zoom. Once a month, before that session, the cohort gathers in person first for a fellowship dinner, then joins the call together. That in-person part is entirely optional; joining by Zoom every time is completely normal.

How much time does this actually take each week?

Most Fellows report around 8–10 hours a week across the whole programme — the fortnightly sessions, daily devotional reading (Monday–Friday), and roughly a book a month, plus two live webinars a month with the books' authors. Reading speed is the biggest variable.

What will I actually study?

The biblical story in full — Creation, Fall, Redemption, Restoration — worked out through roughly a dozen books (Mere Christianity and Knowing God among them), daily devotions, and case-study discussion. See the full programme page for the reading sample and format.

How much does it cost, and why?

Tuition is for the ten months (about ), plus roughly for books, bought separately. Spread across around twenty fortnightly sessions, that works out to a session — for a curated reading list, live conversations with the authors themselves, a small cohort that turns into a genuine community, and a place in a network of more than 6,000 Fellows worldwide.

Scholarships and a four-month payment plan are available. If cost is the only thing standing in your way, email Carol Loi before you rule it out.

Who can apply?

Adults at any stage of life — you'll need to be at least 21 by the August start date. Colson Fellows isn't built around one denomination; the Colson Center describes itself as drawing together, not dividing, the spectrum of Christ-centered people under its published Statement of Faith.

When do applications close, and when does the cohort start?

Priority deadline is , ahead of the start. Applying by then gives everyone — you included — the most time to sort out any questions before the cohort begins.

The priority deadline for this intake has passed, but that doesn't rule you out. Email Carol Loi — there's often still room, and a slightly later start beats waiting a full year.

What happens after I apply?

Your application goes to the Colson Center's admissions team, and the Singapore team is looped in on cohort placement and local details. If anything about next steps is unclear once you've applied, reach out directly for the fastest answer.

What if I can't make the monthly dinner?

No issue at all. It's a bonus for people who want it, not a requirement — you can complete the full programme joining every session by Zoom.

I have more questions — who do I ask?

For Singapore-specific questions: cloi@colsonfellows.org. For general programme questions, the Colson Center's own global FAQ covers policy that applies across every cohort.
