Fellows' Stories

Eleven Fellows. Eleven very different reasons to start.

These are Singapore Fellows, Classes of 2024 through 2026, in their own words — lightly organised, not edited for effect.

Finding the words for something already felt

Not a crisis of belief — a lack of language

"I started the Colson Fellows Program as a homeschooling mum, trying to keep up with the kinds of questions my kids were asking — about truth, identity, and the world they're growing up in. I knew what I believed, but I often found myself not quite knowing how to explain it well. What the Colson Program helped me see was that it wasn't a lack of faith on my part but a lack of language. The program equipped me with a framework to think and speak more clearly, and the confidence to engage hard questions without fear. Now, I don't feel the pressure to have all the answers. Instead, I'm learning to walk with my children, helping them think from a biblical worldview and wrestle honestly, grounded in Scripture. And it's shaped my work too — as a missionary, I've begun to see more clearly the deeper questions people are asking beneath the surface."

Amy Leow — Class of 2026

"Colson has helped me see how important it is to understand and connect with the challenges our generation faces. I've grown a lot in this area. Along the way, I noticed that I often look at God's truth through my own experiences instead of walking life from His perspective. I'm grateful for this, and I encourage anyone looking for insight, growth, and transformation to join this course!"

Jessica — Class of 2026

Beyond the rat race

Depth and rigour, experienced as transformation

"Honestly, the reading almost scared me off. Never read this much in my life but... also never felt this spiritually awaken. What used to feel theoretical suddenly became alive and personal. This wasn't just informational; it was transformational. What led me to join started as a conversation with a friend during a season when I was searching for purpose and feeling like there had to be more to life than the rat race. There had to be, right? It helped me realize God's Word wasn't written only for people thousands of years ago — it speaks into our lives right now. It felt like I've found a compass, and it reoriented me to God's purpose and His Grand Story."

Abrielle — Class of 2026

"What drew me to the programme was the wide-ranging depth of materials I get to dive deep into. I discovered answers to many relevant issues confronting the Christian faith in culture today using a Biblical worldview. I've come to appreciate that the clarity, goodness, and hope that one sees through this transformative lens cannot be 'unseen'. The rigour of the training, in addition to the supportive leadership and the privilege of journeying with fellow Christians who desire to live an unashamedly faithful, purposeful life, has left an indelible impact on me."

Michelle Soon — Class of 2024

Learning to read the culture

Naming the worldviews running quietly underneath

"Here are some of my key takeaways: an opportunity to read widely and deepen my understanding of different worldviews, sharpening my awareness of the cultures and trends shaping the world around me. A platform to hear fellow Christians thoughtfully and courageously articulate a biblical worldview amidst the confusion and complexities of the age we live in. A journey of learning to think critically and develop a biblical framework that helps me discern the underlying ideologies through the lens of God's eternal Word."

Weng Pheng — Class of 2026

"This programme has helped me realise that everyone carries a worldview. As people encounter the challenges of life, even the most pragmatic among them start to ask: what am I here for? Where is all this heading? Christianity is not merely about 'inviting Jesus into my heart,' but about participating in God's loving reign in every part of life. The programme sharpened my discernment — 'ideas have consequences, and bad ideas have victims'. Christians have a protective role: to speak truthfully, reason carefully, and engage culture with courage and compassion."

Charmaine — Class of 2026

"How can Christians influence culture in the midst of a worrying trend of increased polarisation, activism, and unhealthy ideology? I felt an urgency to learn, to understand, and to be equipped. Colson Fellows is a well curated course in terms of the breadth and depth of topics covered, to equip us with a sound Christian worldview to live out our faith in a fractured world. Together, we learn, together we gain courage and hope as we tackle complex issues."

Cheow Lan Ong — Class of 2025

A community that sharpens

"Huddles," and iron sharpening iron

"I started my journey from July 2025. It is a great and classic lifelong learning as I have to restart reading — and more reading, and listening to all the inspired videos and webinars. I am glad that I have signed up and been exposed to a much wider view, to be able to articulate and better express my views as in 1 Peter 3:15. Also, a great bunch of leadership to guide and make our journey much more bearable. It allows fellow students to congregate ('huddles') and spar as iron sharpens iron."

Richard Kwok — Class of 2026

"I am profoundly amazed by God's transformative work in my heart through the devotions, books, videos, and especially the huddles. This experience has reignited my calling in Biblical Worldview ministry, and this journey has fostered a deeper, more intimate relationship with Him. I feel blessed and grateful to be learning alongside a community of like-minded brothers and sisters in Christ, looking forward to serving together, applying the knowledge and wisdom we are gaining."

Dicky — Class of 2026

"I am also extremely grateful for the community within the programme. It has been a safe and encouraging space where I can openly share, learn, and grow. I am continually inspired by the faith, wisdom, and authenticity of my fellow participants and facilitators — I am not merely a spectator in His story, but an active participant called to live out my faith with purpose and conviction."

Angela — Class of 2026

"Being able to see the world through biblical lenses is vital to me. I appreciate that the wide-ranging resources of the programme have helped me understand why we think the way we do, the lies our generation faces, and how we can respond to the culture we're living in today with truth and love. The programme has given me clarity in how I can leverage the strengths God has given me to redeem culture within my influence, starting with my own home."

Olivia Tjasa — Class of 2025
