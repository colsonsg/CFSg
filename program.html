The Programme

Not more information. Formation.

Colson Fellows resists a common mistake thoughtful Christians make: treating knowledge as the same thing as formation. The goal isn't a well-informed Christian. It's a well-formed one — someone whose habits, loves, and instincts have actually been shaped by what they say they believe.

The framework

One Story, told in four movements

Every session, every book, every case study returns to the same frame — commonly shortened to CFRR. It's not a doctrine to memorise. It's the lens Fellows are trained to read everything else through.

01

Creation

The world, and every person in it, was made — on purpose, and for good.

02

Fall

Something is genuinely wrong — not just "out there," but in the ordinary texture of every life.

03

Redemption

Christ's reign is not a private rescue. It reaches into work, culture, and public life.

04

Restoration

History is going somewhere — and Fellows are trained to live now as citizens of where it's headed.

What it actually does

Six things Fellows describe changing

Different Fellows arrive for different reasons. These aren't sequential steps — they're what tends to shift, all at once, over ten months.

See clearly

Recover the full biblical story, and learn to name the competing worldviews — naturalism, consumerism, expressive individualism — running quietly underneath daily life.

Live it on Tuesday

Move faith out of the private sphere. "It wasn't a lack of faith," one Fellow said of the gap she felt — "it was a lack of language." The programme builds the framework, not just the feeling.

Say why, without fear

Several Fellows connect this directly to 1 Peter 3:15 — always ready to give a reasoned account of your hope. Not a debate-club trick. A settled, unhurried confidence.

Sharpen together

Formation doesn't happen alone. Fellows call their cohort discussions "huddles" — a space, as one put it, to "congregate and spar as iron sharpens iron."

Find your particular calling

The final quarter is deliberately practical: a Personal Mission Plan for your own gifting and sphere of influence — not a generic call to "do more."

Hold a durable hope

Not optimism about how the culture is trending. Confidence rooted somewhere that doesn't move when the headlines do.

Curriculum

What you'll actually read

Fellows work through roughly a dozen books over ten months, reviewed and updated annually to stay current. A sample of the foundational titles:

  • Mere Christianity — C.S. Lewis
  • Knowing God — J.I. Packer
  • How Now Shall We Live? — Charles Colson & Nancy Pearcey
  • Restoring All Things — Warren Cole Smith
  • Man's Search for Meaning — Viktor Frankl

The full list, and how it's applied through Singapore-specific case studies, is confirmed with the leadership team ahead of each cohort.

Alongside the reading

  • Daily devotions via an online learning platform — readings, short devotionals, and video.
  • Two live webinars a month with the books' own authors and other Christian thinkers, recordings available if you miss one.
  • Fortnightly cohort discussion — most months, one session on two books, one session pairing a book with a live case study.
  • A Teaching Practicum in the second half — you lead a short worldview overview for a real group. It's assessed on faithful presence, not content mastery.
  • A Personal Mission Plan, developed in the final quarter, applying everything to your own calling.
  • Commissioning, not graduation, on completion.

Singapore format

Built for busy, complex lives — with one deliberate exception

Every fortnight — online

2nd and 4th Thursdays, 8–10pm SGT, over Zoom. Two 50-minute blocks: most months, one session covers two book discussions, the other pairs a book discussion with a case study.

That's already more frequent than the global default of one monthly session — Singapore Fellows get twice the cohort contact.

Once a month — hybrid, optional

Before one session each month, the Singapore cohort gathers in person first — a fellowship dinner — then joins that session together as a group rather than logging in separately.

Entirely optional. If you can't make it, or prefer to join by Zoom every time, that's a completely normal way to do this programme.

Outside those sessions, the reading and daily devotions are entirely self-paced — most Fellows report spending 8–10 hours a week on the whole programme, reading speed being the biggest variable.

Who it's for

Adults at any stage of life

Colson Fellows isn't built for pastors specifically, or for any one denomination. Singapore Fellows have included homeschooling parents, missionaries, business owners, and people newly wrestling with faith for the first time in years. You'll need to be at least 21 by the cohort's August start date. Beyond that, the common thread is simpler than a résumé: a sense that something more is being asked of you than private belief.

Part of something larger

A global network of Fellows

Colson Fellows runs internationally, with Singapore as one cohort inside a much wider network — including active cohorts across the U.S., Canada, South Korea, Australia, and beyond. The Colson Center reports more than 6,000 men and women commissioned worldwide since the programme began.
