If any of this sounds familiar

Four patterns we keep seeing

Knowing the right answers isn't the same as reading the room.

Every generation is taught two things at once: what to believe on Sunday, and what to notice the rest of the week. Almost nobody is trained in the second one. "Colson has helped me see how important it is to understand and connect with the challenges our generation faces," one Fellow said — after years of church attendance, not before them.

Your kids are asking better questions than you were ever taught to answer.

Faith has always passed down through explanation, not just example. A Singapore Fellow and homeschooling mother put it plainly: what she was missing "wasn't a lack of faith on my part but a lack of language" — a framework, not a firmer conviction.

A life measured only in output eventually asks you an uncomfortable question.

Push achievement far enough as an operating system for a life and it stops delivering meaning, even when it keeps delivering results. "There had to be more to life than the rat race" is how one Fellow remembers the season that led her to apply.

The culture isn't waiting for anyone to catch up.

Ideas about truth, identity, and meaning move fast, and most people are handed strong opinions long before they're handed the tools to think one through. Fellows consistently describe wanting to engage that speed with clarity and love — not silence, and not fear.

What Colson Fellows is

The Story that makes sense of every other story

Colson Fellows is a formation programme, not an information course. At its centre is the biblical story in full — Creation, Fall, Redemption, Restoration — not doctrine to affirm on Sundays, but the lens Fellows are trained to actually read Monday through Saturday with.

Fellows work through a curated reading list alongside daily devotions and twice-monthly live webinars with the books' own authors, in a small cohort that reads, discusses, and — in the words several Fellows have used independently of each other — sharpens one another.

It ends in commissioning, not graduation: a deliberate marker that formation is for the sake of a life lived differently, not a certificate.

The programme, in three words

Clarity

To see the world as it actually is, not as competing stories about meaning and identity present it.

Confidence

To give, as 1 Peter 3:15 puts it, a reasoned account of the hope you carry — without needing every answer first.

Courage

To act on your calling, whatever it costs, because the hope on offer isn't optimism about circumstances.

How it works

Ten months, in four movements

AUG

Cohort begins

Daily devotions and reading start, alongside your first fortnightly session.

SEP–JAN

Reading & formation

~12–14 books, twice-monthly author webinars, fortnightly cohort discussion.

JAN–APR

Practicum & Mission Plan

You teach a short worldview overview, and build a Personal Mission Plan for your own calling.

MAY

Commissioning

You join a global network of Fellows commissioned, not merely graduated.

Singapore format

The Singapore cohort meets twice a month, Thursday evenings, 8–10pm SGT, online — already more often than the global default of one monthly session. Once a month, before that session, the cohort gathers in person first for a fellowship dinner, then joins the call together as a group rather than each logging in from home.

Entirely optional — plenty of Fellows join by Zoom every time — but it's the part many say they'd miss most.
