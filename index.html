Four ways Fellows describe arriving here

You can recite the doctrine. Can you read the culture?

Fellows describe a moment of realising that everyone operates from a worldview — including the ideas that never announce themselves as one. "Colson has helped me see how important it is to understand and connect with the challenges our generation faces," one 2026 Fellow put it.

Your kids are asking questions you can't quite answer.

A Singapore Fellow and homeschooling mother described trying to keep up with what her children were asking about truth and identity. What she was missing, she realised, "wasn't a lack of faith on my part but a lack of language."

Something in you is tired of measuring life in achievements.

"There had to be more to life than the rat race" is how one 2026 Fellow describes the season that led her to apply. She wasn't in a crisis of belief — she was searching for something belief hadn't yet reached.

The ground keeps shifting under words like truth and identity.

Fellows consistently describe wanting to engage a fast-changing culture with clarity and love — not silence, and not fear. Several call the community itself, not just the reading, where that courage actually took root.

What Colson Fellows is

The Story that makes sense of every other story

Colson Fellows is a formation programme, not an information course. At its centre is the biblical story in full — Creation, Fall, Redemption, Restoration — not as doctrine to affirm on Sundays, but as the lens Fellows are trained to actually read Monday through Saturday with: a headline, a performance review, a disagreement with a friend.

Fellows work through a curated reading list alongside daily devotions and twice-monthly live webinars with the books' own authors, in a small cohort that reads, discusses, and — in the words several Fellows have used independently of each other — sharpens one another.

It ends in commissioning, not graduation: a deliberate marker that formation is for the sake of a life lived differently, not a certificate.

The programme, in three words

Clarity

To see the world as it actually is, not as competing stories about meaning and identity present it.

Confidence

To give, as 1 Peter 3:15 puts it, a reasoned account of the hope you carry — without needing to have every answer first.

Courage

To act faithfully in your own calling, whatever it costs, because the hope on offer isn't optimism about circumstances.

How it works

Ten months, in four movements

AUG

Cohort begins

Daily devotions and reading start, alongside your first fortnightly session.

SEP–JAN

Reading & formation

~12–14 books, twice-monthly author webinars, fortnightly cohort discussion.

JAN–APR

Practicum & Mission Plan

You teach a short worldview overview, and build a Personal Mission Plan for your own calling.

MAY

Commissioning

You join a global network of Fellows commissioned, not merely graduated.

Singapore format

The Singapore cohort meets twice a month, Thursday evenings, 8–10pm SGT, online — already more often than the global default of one monthly session. Once a month, before that session, the Singapore cohort gathers in person first for a fellowship dinner, then joins the call together as a group rather than each logging in from home.

Entirely optional — plenty of Fellows join by Zoom every time — but it's the part many say they'd miss most.
